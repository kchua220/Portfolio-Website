Global Suicide Trends: Gender Disparities and Insights
 Group Project

This UW INFO 201 project examines global suicide trends through comprehensive data analysis. We collaborated and explored gender disparities, country-specific patterns, and socio-economic factors influencing suicide rates using datasets from the World Health Organization (WHO). By leveraging tools that we learned like R, ggplot2, dplyr, and Plotly, we conducted statistical analyses and created interactive visualizations to uncover key insights. The findings aim to inform targeted mental health interventions and highlight strategies addressing these critical global challenges, emphasizing the need for data-driven solutions in mental health policymaking.

Global Pathways: OPT/CPT Made Easy
 Group Project

This INFO 200 project focuses on supporting international students at the University of Washington by addressing challenges in navigating OPT (Optional Practical Training) and CPT (Curricular Practical Training) internship processes. We identified gaps in accessible and tailored resources, often leaving international students at a disadvantage. Through surveys, interviews, and research, we created an interactive and customizable Notion template as our solution. This template includes features such as job application trackers, networking tools, OPT/CPT checklists, educational resources, and even a Mandarin translation page, offering an all-in-one platform to streamline internship searches and bridge equity gaps in the job market.
